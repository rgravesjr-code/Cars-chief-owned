1986 Porsche 944 Turbo
The 944 Turbo S — one of Porsche's finest
Year: 1986  ·  Miles: 43,000  ·  Engine: 2.5L Turbocharged 4-cyl  ·  Output: ~220 hp

The Porsche 944 Turbo was one of the great sports cars of the 1980s — a genuinely capable machine that combined Porsche engineering precision with the raw performance boost of forced induction. Roger acquired this excellent 43,000-mile example in 1995, and by all accounts it delivered on every front. With around 220 horsepower from its turbocharged 2.5-litre four-cylinder, it was genuinely fast — something Roger noted approvingly.

The 944 Turbo (internally the 951) was a revelation when launched — 50/50 weight distribution courtesy of the rear transaxle, sophisticated suspension, and performance that embarrassed many more exotic machinery. Road & Track called it one of the best-handling cars in the world, and Car and Driver regularly pitted it against Ferraris. Roger also owned a 1986 Porsche 944 (non-turbo) champagne model in the family car section — showing a real appreciation for the 944 platform.

This was the kind of car that rewarded a skilled driver enormously, and Roger's decision to acquire an excellent low-mileage example showed his eye for a quality buy. The 944 Turbo remains one of the most underrated performance cars of its era — a Porsche that delivered 911-adjacent performance at a fraction of the price.
