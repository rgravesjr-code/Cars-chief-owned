Triumph TR-3 Roadster

Brand new — took on the honeymoon in 1962

🏎️
1963 Triumph TR-3
Classic British roadster
Year: 1963  ·  Acquired: 1962 (brand new)  ·  Engine: 2.0L 4-cyl  ·  Category: Sports Roadster

Few cars carry the emotional weight of a honeymoon vehicle, and Roger's 1963 Triumph TR-3 Roadster is just that — bought brand new and taken on the most important journey of his life. The TR-3 was Triumph's beloved two-seat roadster, a proper old-school sports car with a separate-chassis design, a muscular 2.0-litre four-cylinder engine, and enough character to fill a room.

The TR-3 was already a legend by the time Roger acquired his. First introduced in 1955, it was one of the first British production cars to offer front disc brakes, giving it a genuine performance edge over its contemporaries. The wide-set front grille, long hood, and cutaway doors made it unmistakably purposeful — a car designed to cover ground quickly and look magnificent doing it.

Taking a brand-new TR-3 on a honeymoon speaks volumes about the man — an early sign of a life lived with an eye for the special, the spirited, and the memorable. The TR-3 started Roger and Diane's life together with the wind in their hair and a proper British gearbox in hand. There would be many more great cars to come, but none more significant than this one.
