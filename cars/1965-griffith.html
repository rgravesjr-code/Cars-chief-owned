Griffith Series 400

289 HiPo V8 · 0–60 in 4.6 seconds · 1,500 lbs · The fastest car Roger ever owned

Year: 1965  ·  Engine: Ford 289 HiPo V8  ·  Weight: 1,500 lbs  ·  0–60: 4.6 seconds  ·  Status: New — Rare

The 1965 Griffith Series 400 holds a unique place in Roger's collection: it is the fastest car he ever owned. Built by Jack Griffith in New York, the Griffith was a raw, brutal Anglo-American special — a lightweight British TVR body stuffed with Ford's legendary 289 High Performance V8. At just 1,500 lbs, the power-to-weight ratio was staggering, and the 0–60 time of 4.6 seconds was supercar territory in 1965.

The Griffith was a limited-production machine — fewer than 600 were ever built — making it genuinely rare even when new. Jack Griffith had taken his inspiration from Carroll Shelby's Cobra concept: take a light British sports car, drop in a big American V8, and create something terrifying and wonderful. The result was a car with almost no creature comforts but an abundance of visceral, white-knuckle performance that few road cars of any era could match.

For Roger to acquire one new in 1965 was remarkable. The Griffith was not an easy car — it demanded respect and rewarded commitment. The 289 HiPo engine that powered it was the same unit found in Shelby Mustangs and early Cobras, and in a car weighing less than most modern motorcycles, it was utterly transformative. No car in the collection before or since matched the Griffith for sheer seat-of-the-pants speed.
