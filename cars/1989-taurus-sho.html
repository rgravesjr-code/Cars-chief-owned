Ford Taurus SHO

Dark red · Nice · 1990

Year: 1989  ·  Color: Dark Red  ·  Engine: Yamaha 3.0L V6 · 220hp  ·  Trans: 5-Speed Manual

The Ford Taurus SHO — Super High Output — was one of the great sleepers of the late 1980s. It looked exactly like a regular Taurus family sedan, but under the hood lurked a 220-horsepower Yamaha-designed 3.0-litre V6 mated to a Mazda 5-speed manual gearbox. The result was a family car that could out-accelerate nearly everything in its price class, reaching 60 mph in under 7 seconds when most family sedans were closer to 11.

Car & Driver called the SHO "the best American sedan" when it launched, and Roger's dark red example was acquired in 1990 — early enough to get the most exciting version with the proper 5-speed manual. The SHO was Roger's kind of car: practical enough for family duties, but with enough performance to keep things interesting. It was rated "nice" in his notes, which for a family sedan from Roger is high praise indeed.
