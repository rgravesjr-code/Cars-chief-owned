Honda Super Hawk

Brand new · Rode to Niagara Falls · 1965

Year: 1965  ·  Engine: 305cc parallel twin  ·  Purchased: New · 1965

The Honda CB77 Super Hawk was Honda's flagship sports motorcycle of the mid-1960s — a 305cc twin-cylinder machine that combined genuine performance with Honda's legendary reliability. Roger bought one brand new in 1965, the same year he also acquired a new BSA Lightning, suggesting a particularly motorcycle-enthusiastic period in his life. The CB77 was the bike that proved Japanese motorcycles could compete with the best British machines on performance terms.

Roger rode this Super Hawk all the way to Niagara Falls — a proper long-distance tour that demonstrated both his adventurous spirit and the CB77's touring credentials. The Super Hawk was rev-happy, well-balanced, and smooth in a way that British bikes of the era often were not. It was the beginning of Roger's long relationship with Honda motorcycles that would span from this CB77 through multiple CB750s and several Goldwings.
