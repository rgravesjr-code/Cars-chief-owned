Year: 1959  ·  Color: Brown  ·  Acquired: 1964  ·  Condition: Fair

Roger's second Austin Healey Sprite was this brown example with a custom front end — a departure from the famous fixed "Bugeye" headlights of the standard car. Someone had modified the nose, giving it a more conventional look with opening panels. It was a fair-condition car acquired in 1964, a year after the white Sprite had set the hook for British sports car ownership.

The Sprite's appeal was timeless: lightweight, honest, and genuinely fun. Even in brown with custom bodywork, it embodied everything Roger loved about small British sports cars — direct steering, a responsive chassis, and a character that no modern car could replicate. This was the second in what would become a remarkable run of Austin Healeys through the mid-1960s.
