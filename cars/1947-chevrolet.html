Year: 1947  ·  Color: Black  ·  Price: $125  ·  Purchased: 1957 · US Navy

Right alongside the 1946 Pontiac, Roger picked up this black 1947 Chevrolet for $125 while serving in the US Navy in 1957. Two cars for $240 total — practical thinking from a young serviceman who wanted options. The post-war Chevrolet was a solid, dependable machine: a wide, upright body with a Blue Flame inline six or optional V8, built when American cars were genuinely simple and repairable by anyone with basic tools.

The 1947 Chevy represented the tail end of the pre-war styling era, before the dramatic fins and chrome excess of the 1950s transformed American car design. In black, it would have cut a formal, purposeful figure. Like the Pontiac, it served its purpose and gave Roger the mobility and freedom a young sailor needed when off duty.
