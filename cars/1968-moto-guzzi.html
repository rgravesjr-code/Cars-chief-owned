Moto Guzzi 700

Rode to the Great Smoky Mountains · 1969

Year: 1968  ·  Engine: 700cc transverse V-twin  ·  Rode to: Great Smoky Mountains · 1969

The Moto Guzzi 700 was a rare and distinguished choice — an Italian transverse V-twin in an era when most American riders chose British or Japanese machines. The Moto Guzzi V7 series used a longitudinally-mounted transverse V-twin engine that drove through a shaft rather than a chain, making it uniquely smooth and requiring virtually no drivetrain maintenance. It was a sophisticated, European machine that stood apart from everything else on the road.

Roger rode this Guzzi all the way to the Great Smoky Mountains in 1969 — one of America's great motorcycle touring destinations. The winding roads through Tennessee and North Carolina were a perfect match for the Guzzi's character: a long-legged, smooth-cruising machine that ate up miles without fuss. Taking an Italian transverse V-twin to the Smokies in 1969 was the act of a true motorcycling adventurer.
