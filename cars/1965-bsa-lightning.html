BSA A65L
The classic British parallel twin
Year: 1965  ·  Engine: 650cc parallel twin  ·  Purchased: New · 1965

In 1965, Roger owned both a Honda Super Hawk and this brand-new BSA Lightning — an enviable pair that represented the very best of Japanese and British motorcycle engineering side by side. The BSA A65L Lightning was BSA's performance twin, fitted with twin Amal carburetors and producing around 52 horsepower from its 650cc parallel twin engine. It was fast, characterful, and unmistakably British.

The BSA Lightning represented the golden age of the British motorcycle industry — before Japanese reliability and refinement would eventually overwhelm the traditional manufacturers. The thumping parallel twin, the distinctive exhaust note, the smell of Castrol R: owning a new BSA Lightning in 1965 was to be at the very heart of motorcycling culture. Roger's simultaneous ownership of the smooth Japanese Honda and the characterful British BSA showed his appreciation for two entirely different motorcycling philosophies.
