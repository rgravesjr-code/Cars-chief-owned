Honda Goldwing GL1000

Red · 1983 · First of three Goldwings owned

Year: 1976  ·  Color: Red  ·  Engine: 1000cc flat-four  ·  Acquired: 1983

The Honda Goldwing GL1000 launched in 1975 was one of the most ambitious motorcycles Honda had ever built — a water-cooled, shaft-driven flat-four engine in a full-size touring motorcycle that redefined what long-distance two-wheel travel could be. Roger acquired this red 1976 example in 1983, the first of three Goldwings he would own over the years, showing a genuine appreciation for Honda's touring flagship.

The original GL1000 Goldwing was a revelation: smooth, powerful, utterly refined compared to the air-cooled machines of the era. Honda had essentially taken automobile engine technology and applied it to a motorcycle, creating something that cruised effortlessly at highway speeds all day without the vibration and heat of a conventional engine. In red, it was a handsome machine — and the beginning of a Goldwing ownership story that would span three examples and more than a decade.
