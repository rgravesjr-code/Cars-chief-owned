Harley-Davidson Heritage Springer

White · Brand new · Beautiful · "My favorite model" — owned three examples

Year: 1997  ·  Color: White  ·  Purchased: New in 1997  ·  Significance: First of three owned

The Harley-Davidson Heritage Springer is the motorcycle Roger called his favorite model — a distinction he backed up by owning three examples over the years. The Heritage Springer combined the classic "springer" front fork, a throwback to pre-hydraulic suspension design, with the comfortable Heritage Softail frame. The result was a motorcycle that looked like it had ridden straight out of 1948 but with modern Harley reliability underneath.

Roger bought this white example brand new in 1997 — the same year he sold a Mazda Miata to fund the purchase, showing just how serious he was about getting on two wheels again. The Heritage Springer was not a sport bike; it was a rolling piece of American cultural history, all chrome and leather and the unmistakable potato-potato idle of the Evolution V-twin. Beautiful is the right word for it.

Over the years Roger would return to this model twice more — buying a second Heritage Springer in 2006 via eBay, and a third in 2009, which he noted was "most likely my last motorcycle." When a man returns to the same model three times, it tells you everything about what the motorcycle meant to him.
