Austin Healey Sprite

White — my very first sports car, acquired in 1961

Year: 1959  ·  Color: White  ·  Acquired: 1961  ·  Category: Sports Car

The 1959 Austin Healey Sprite — affectionately known as the "Bugeye" or "Frogeye" Sprite for its distinctive fixed headlights mounted atop the hood — was Roger's very first sports car. Acquired in 1961, this little gem introduced him to the pure joy of open-top motoring that would define a lifetime of collecting. At just 1,300 lbs and powered by a spirited 948cc BMC A-Series engine, the Sprite was light, nimble, and grin-inducing at every turn.

The Sprite was Donald Healey's answer to an affordable British sports car, launched in 1958. Its unitary body construction, rack-and-pinion steering, and independent front suspension made it genuinely fun to drive — a real driver's car despite its tiny engine. The fixed headlights, a cost-cutting measure, became the model's most iconic feature and gave the car an almost cartoon-like face that made it instantly loveable.

For Roger, the white Sprite was the beginning of a love affair with open-top British motoring that would continue for years — through TR-3s, MGBs, Austin Healey 100-6s and beyond. The Bugeye Sprite was where it all started: the wind in your hair, a proper gearchange, and the unmistakable sound of a small British engine singing at full chat.
