Honda CB750
Brand new in 1970
Year: 1970  ·  Engine: 736cc Inline-4  ·  Purchased: New  ·  Company: Larry Nevers

The 1970 Honda CB750 was not just a great motorcycle — it was arguably the most important motorcycle of the 20th century. When Honda launched the CB750 in 1969, it redefined what a motorcycle could be: a disc-braked, inline four-cylinder machine that was faster, smoother, and more reliable than anything the British or American competition could offer. Roger bought one brand new in 1970, joining thousands of motorcyclists who recognised something genuinely revolutionary.

Roger rode his CB750 extensively with friend Larry Nevers — a proper riding partnership on what was then the pinnacle of production motorcycle technology. The CB750's four-cylinder engine produced a silky 67 horsepower, its disc front brake was a first for a production motorcycle, and its overall refinement put the entire industry on notice. Kawasaki, Suzuki, and Yamaha all scrambled to respond, launching the golden age of the Japanese inline four.

In the same year, Roger also bought a Honda 350 Enduro and a Honda 100 Enduro for Diane — showing that motorcycling had become a true family pursuit. The CB750 was the crown jewel of that collection.
