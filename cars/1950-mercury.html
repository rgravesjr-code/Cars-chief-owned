Year: 1950  ·  Engine: Buick (modified)  ·  Induction: Six 1-barrel carburetors  ·  Origin: Jerry Brooks

This 1950 Mercury was no ordinary car — it had been fitted with a suped-up Buick engine breathing through six single-barrel carburetors, making it a genuine hot rod in the classic American tradition. The car came from Jerry Brooks and was eventually sold to Larry Dumas, suggesting it passed through a community of enthusiasts who appreciated its modified character.

The 1950 Mercury was already a beloved canvas for customizers — James Dean immortalized the model in Rebel Without a Cause, and lead sleds based on the '49-'51 Mercury were everywhere in the custom car world. A Buick-engined example with six carbs would have been a serious street performer, producing power far beyond the stock flathead Ford V8. This was Roger's early taste of performance modification — a thread that would run through sports cars, Corvettes, and beyond.
