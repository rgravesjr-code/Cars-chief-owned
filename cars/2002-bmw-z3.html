BMW Z3 3.0i

Red · 5-speed · Only 27,000 miles — acquired 2009

Year: 2002  ·  Color: Red  ·  Engine: 3.0L Inline-6  ·  Trans: 5-Speed Manual  ·  Miles: 27,000

Roger owned two BMW Z3s, and this red 2002 3.0i was the pick of the pair. Where the earlier 1998 Z3 2.8 was already a strong car, the 3.0i variant pushed it further — 231 horsepower from BMW's silky M54 inline six, making it the most powerful non-M Z3 available. At just 27,000 miles when Roger acquired it in 2009, this was an exceptionally well-preserved example of BMW's roadster at its finest.

The Z3 3.0i was the last and best evolution of the standard Z3 — wider rear bodywork, stiffened chassis, and an engine that combined lusty low-end torque with a magnificent high-rpm soundtrack. The 5-speed manual gearbox gave it a direct, involving feel that matched the car's sporting character perfectly. In red, it was a head-turner that combined German precision with genuine open-air excitement.

Roger's Z3 journey had started with the white 2.8 in 2002 and ended with this red 3.0i — a natural progression to the better car. The photo in the collection shows it circa 2007, top down on a beautiful day: exactly what a BMW Z3 was made for.
