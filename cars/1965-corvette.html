Corvette Roadster

Dark green · 327 V8 · 4-speed · My first Vet

Year: 1965  ·  Color: Dark Green  ·  Engine: 327 V8  ·  Trans: 4-Speed Manual  ·  Body: Roadster

The 1965 Corvette Roadster in dark green was the first of many Corvettes Roger would own over the decades — a love affair with America's sports car that would span more than 45 years. The C2 Sting Ray, as the 1963–1967 generation is known, is widely considered the most beautiful Corvette ever made, and the 1965 model was among the finest: the year Chevrolet introduced four-wheel disc brakes and refined the independent rear suspension that had debuted in 1963.

The 327 cubic inch V8 with a close-ratio 4-speed manual was a magnificent combination — crisp throttle response, a mechanical symphony through the gears, and enough torque to embarrass almost anything on the road. In dark green with a matching convertible top down, this was a car that turned heads everywhere it went, equal parts muscle and elegance in a way that no other American car of the era could quite match.

This first Corvette set the template for what Roger looked for in all the Vettes that followed: low miles, excellent condition, the right colour, and that unmistakable V8 soundtrack. From this dark green roadster in 1966 through to the last Corvette in 2011, the marque remained a constant thread through one of the great personal car collections of the post-war era.
