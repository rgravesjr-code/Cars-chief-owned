American luxury at its finest
Year: 1991  ·  Color: Titanium  ·  Interior: Grey Leather  ·  Acquired: 1996

The 1991 Lincoln Continental represented American luxury at its most refined — a front-wheel-drive executive sedan with a 3.8-litre V6, air suspension, and every comfort feature Ford's engineers could devise. Roger's titanium example with grey leather interior was the fully-loaded variant, and it showed his eye for quality over flash. Titanium with grey leather is a restrained, sophisticated combination — understated elegance rather than showmanship.

The eighth-generation Continental was a significant car for Lincoln: modern, aerodynamic, and technologically advanced compared to the body-on-frame Continentals of earlier decades. The air suspension, automatic climate control, and power everything made it genuinely comfortable over long distances. This was a car for a man who appreciated quality craftsmanship — and Roger owned several Lincolns over the years, suggesting a real appreciation for the marque.
