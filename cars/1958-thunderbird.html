The 4-seat T-Bird era
Year: 1958  ·  Color: Blue  ·  Acquired: 1961  ·  Context: First car as a civilian

After his Navy years, Roger's first car as a civilian was this blue 1958 Thunderbird — a significant step up from the modest post-war Chevys and Pontiacs that had served him in uniform. The 1958 T-Bird represented a pivotal moment in the Thunderbird's history: Ford had transformed it from the elegant two-seat original into a larger four-passenger "Square Bird," offering genuine practicality alongside the T-Bird's prestige.

The 1958 Thunderbird was powered by a 352 cubic inch FE-series V8, making it a genuinely powerful car for its era. The distinctive "Squarebird" bodywork with its prominent fins, wide C-pillar, and porthole windows was unmistakably American luxury of the late 1950s. As a first civilian car, it showed that Roger's tastes ran to something more characterful than the ordinary — a pattern that would define six decades of remarkable automotive collecting.
