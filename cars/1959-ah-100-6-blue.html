Austin Healey 100-6

Blue — acquired 1965

Year: 1959  ·  Color: Blue  ·  Acquired: 1965  ·  Condition: Fair

The Austin Healey 100-6 — known affectionately as the "Big Healey" — was a significant step up from the Sprite. Powered by a 2.6-litre inline six-cylinder engine, the 100-6 offered genuine long-distance touring ability combined with classic British sports car character. This blue example was Roger's first of what would become two 100-6s owned in 1965 alone.

The 100-6 replaced the original four-cylinder 100 in 1956, adding two cylinders and a longer wheelbase for occasional rear seating. Despite being rated only fair in Roger's notes, the 100-6 remains one of the most handsome cars Donald Healey ever penned — wide, low, and purposeful, with a long hood that hinted at the power underneath.
