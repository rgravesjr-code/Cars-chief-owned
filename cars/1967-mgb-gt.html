MGB GT

Blue — blew second gear in 1970

Year: 1967  ·  Color: Blue  ·  Acquired: 1970  ·  Fate: Blew second gear

The MGB GT was MG's elegant fastback coupé version of the beloved MGB roadster — a Pininfarina-styled 2+2 that brought practicality to the sports car formula without sacrificing character. Roger's blue 1967 GT was photographed with son Randy, a charming family snapshot of the car in everyday use. The GT's hatchback design and fold-down rear seat made it unusually versatile for a British sports car of the era.

The MGB GT was a refined companion but its tenure ended in a classic British sports car fashion — a blown second gear. Gearbox fragility was an occupational hazard of driving British cars hard, and this GT eventually succumbed. Still, it was part of Roger's affection for British machinery that stretched from his first Sprite through the late 1980s MGBs.
