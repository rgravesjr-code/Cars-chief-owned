Mercury Convertible

Red · "Tragedy" · In the Navy · Met Diane · 1960

Year: 1955  ·  Color: Red  ·  Nickname: "Tragedy"  ·  Year Acquired: 1960

Roger nicknamed this red 1955 Mercury Convertible "Tragedy" — but whatever misfortune the car may have brought in one sense, it also brought something extraordinary: it was the car in which Roger met Diane. The 1955 Mercury was one of the great American cars of the era, a longer and more stylish companion to the Ford, with swooping lines, a V8 engine, and the kind of dramatic convertible proportions that made everything look like a movie.

A red convertible in 1960 was about as romantic a vehicle as existed on American roads. The 1955 Mercury wore its Virgil Exner-influenced styling with confidence — wide, low, and unmistakably mid-century American. Whatever "Tragedy" referred to in Roger's notes, the car's most important contribution to this story was introducing him to Diane, who would become his wife and travel companion for decades of road trips, adventures, and cars beyond counting.

Some cars matter not for what they are but for what happens in them. The red Mercury convertible is one of those cars.
