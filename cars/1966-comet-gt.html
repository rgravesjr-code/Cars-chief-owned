Mercury Comet GT

289 V8 · 4-speed · 1967

Year: 1966–1968  ·  Engine: 289 V8  ·  Trans: 4-Speed Manual

The Mercury Comet GT was the sporty variant of Ford's compact Comet line — and with the 289 cubic inch V8 and four-speed manual gearbox, it was a legitimately quick machine. The GT package gave the Comet bucket seats, a floor shifter, and performance-oriented trim that made it stand out from the ordinary family Comets. Roger's example, shown in the photo, was a sharp-looking car with genuine performance credentials.

The 289 V8 was the same basic engine family that powered early Mustangs and Shelby Cobras — a rev-happy, high-revving small-block that transformed the lightweight Comet into something genuinely exciting to drive. In an era when the muscle car wars were heating up, the Comet GT punched well above its weight class and price point.
