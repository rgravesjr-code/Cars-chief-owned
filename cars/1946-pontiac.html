1946 Pontiac

Black · $115 · The very first car · US Navy, 1957

Year: 1946  ·  Color: Black  ·  Price: $115  ·  Year Purchased: 1957  ·  Context: US Navy

Every great collection has a beginning, and Roger's began with a black 1946 Pontiac bought for $115 while serving in the United States Navy in 1957. A post-war Pontiac would have been a solid, substantial machine — broad-shouldered, chrome-trimmed, with the kind of presence that post-war American cars had in abundance. At $115, it was an accessible entry into car ownership for a young serviceman.

The 1946 Pontiac was part of the first wave of post-war American production, essentially a refreshed pre-war design as Detroit ramped back up from wartime manufacturing. But it was a real car, with a proper inline six or eight-cylinder engine, and it gave Roger his first taste of the freedom that comes with having four wheels of your own. From this $115 starting point, an extraordinary automotive life was launched.
